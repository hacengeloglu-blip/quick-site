How usage works.

Quick uses a weekly question budget. No daily resets, no waiting, no cooldowns between questions.

Your weekly cap

Every plan gets a generous weekly question budget that resets every seven days on a rolling window — there's no fixed reset day, just a clean seven-day clock from your first question of the cycle.

  • Free — 20 questions / week
  • Pro — 100 questions / week
  • Max — 400 questions / week

A "question" is one prompt that calls Quick and streams an answer back. Most questions take three seconds. Long-form answers count as one question regardless of length.

What happens at the limit

Below your cap: full speed.

Approaching your cap (above 80%): a small in-app banner reminds you.

At your cap: Quick switches to a faster, lighter model so you can keep going. You don't get blocked mid-question — ever.

This is a soft cap, not a hard wall. Most people never see it.

Fair use

Max gets a generous 400-questions-per-week cap because most users land closer to a hundred. Sustained heavy use beyond that may be throttled to keep the service fast for everyone. We'll never bill you a surprise fee — at worst, your speed drops slightly until the week resets.

Why questions, not tokens

Some AI apps measure usage in "tokens" — fragments of words. We think that's confusing. We measure in questions because that's what you actually do: ask a question, get an answer.

Internally we still track tokens to keep costs honest, but you'll never see the number. The question count is what matters.

Reset cadence

Your week is a rolling seven-day window. If you sent five questions last Monday, that bucket frees up next Monday — no waiting until the first of the month.

You can see your current usage anytime in the Quick app, under Settings → Usage.
